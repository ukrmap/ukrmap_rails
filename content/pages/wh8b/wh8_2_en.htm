3. Implications and significance of the Great geographical discoveries

Geographical discoveries had huge impact on economic life across Europe. If previously Major trade routes ran across the Mediterranean and the Baltic Sea, they are now shifted to the oceans. An world trade; that configured connections between different continent. In open continents, to colonies carried articles of iron, weapons, textiles and more. To Europe from America Asia and brought tobacco, coffee, tea, cocoa, spices, gold, silver, jewelry. Began forming the world market as part of the economy of the new society.

Place Italy as the most advanced trading country in Europe took the first Portugal and Spain, and later - the Netherlands and England. New center of business activity are the following European cities as Cadiz  and Seville in Spain, Antwerp and Amsterdam in the Netherlands, London in England.

Europeans have learned amazing grow crops - corn, tobacco, tomato. Use in food potatoes, maize, sugar, ground nuts, tropical fruits, meat, turkey proved very useful. Besides food resources continent doubled.  With the spread of potato Europeans finally got rid of the threat of starvation. Corn was nutritious food for people and good fodder. The development of sugar production in Brazil and its export Europe have led to the emergence of new sectors of the food industry. Sweets become available not only wealthy but also ordinary people. On the Europeans came various fish dishes.

The immediate The discovery was also creation colonial system. The first created a colonial empire and Portugal Spain, later - Holland, England and France. Possession of colonies brought Member colonizers huge profits and contributed to the development of their economy.

One of the major consequences was the Age of Discovery "Price revolution". In the 40's of the XVI century. price of many commodities began rapidly grow, surprising and revolting accustomed to steady prices in Europe. Price bread in the XVI century. increased 5-7 times. Europeans accused of buyers, speculators in high prices, but the cause of high prices was that number of gold and silver, imported from America to Europe and India, was extremely large. Estimates to 1600 it was about 186 t gold and 16 thousand t silver. This led to a significant reduction of gold and silver money and corresponding increase in commodity prices, which, in turn, led to significant and cost of living.

In Europe Prices increased in different ways. The largest and the fastest they have grown in Spain end of the XVI century. exceeding five times the price beginning of the century.
